Questions

Frequently asked

Can someone write about me without my consent?

No. The Wall never creates a profile for someone who hasn't joined. If a contact drafts feedback for you before you join, it's encrypted and held briefly in escrow — only released to you after you join and consent, and even then it stays private until you choose to share it.

Is feedback anonymous?

For someone you invite, you always send the invite yourself, so you're identified. For existing members, reviewers can choose to be named or anonymous to other users. In all cases we retain a recoverable identity mapping so we can comply with lawful requests — anonymous never means anonymous to the law.

How do you prevent abuse?

Feedback uses fixed rating dimensions and curated tags — no free-form slurs. Comments pass two-layer moderation. You can block, report, and dispute, and aggregates only appear after a minimum number of reviews to protect against single-source attacks.

What does it cost?

The core experience is free. Premium unlocks deep personal analytics, trend charts, coaching prompts, and feedback campaigns.

Where is my data stored?

In India (Google Cloud, Mumbai region), consistent with DPDP transfer rules. You can export or permanently delete it anytime from Settings.
