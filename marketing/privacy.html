Privacy Policy

Effective 10 June 2026

Known is built consent-first. We process your personal data only with your consent, and our practices are designed to meet the major global privacy regimes — the EU/UK General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA), and India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP). This policy explains what we collect, why, and the rights you have wherever you live.

1. What we collect

  • Your phone number — to authenticate you and to derive a hashed identity for matching feedback.
  • Your display name and the structured feedback you give and receive (ratings, tags, optional comments).
  • A device push token, to notify you about new feedback.
  • Basic, pseudonymised usage analytics and crash diagnostics to improve the app — collected only after you consent, and switchable off anytime in Settings.

2. What we never collect

We do not store your raw address book. Contacts are hashed on your device, and we never create a profile for someone who has not joined and consented. No wall exists for a non-member.

3. Legal basis

We rely on your explicit, withdrawable consent, captured at sign-up (GDPR Art. 6(1)(a) and 7; DPDP s. 6; and your right to opt out under the CCPA/CPRA). You may withdraw consent at any time by deleting your account. We do not sell or share your personal information for cross-context behavioural advertising.

4. How feedback visibility works

Feedback others give you stays private until you choose to make it public on your Wall. A reviewer may appear "anonymous" to other users; however, we retain a recoverable identity mapping so we can comply with lawful requests from competent authorities (including, in India, under the IT Act). Anonymous never means anonymous to law enforcement.

5. Where your data is stored & international transfers

Your data is hosted on Google Cloud, encrypted in transit and at rest, currently in the Mumbai (asia-south1) region. If you access Known from outside India, your data is transferred to and processed in India. Where required, such transfers are protected by appropriate safeguards — including Standard Contractual Clauses for users in the EU/UK — and India is not on any restricted-transfer list under the DPDP Act.

6. Retention

Feedback drafted for someone who has not yet joined is encrypted in escrow and auto-deleted after 30 days if they do not join. When you delete your account, your profile, your Wall, and feedback about you are permanently erased.

7. Your rights

Wherever you live, you may access and export your data, request correction, withdraw consent, and erase your account — all from Settings. Depending on your jurisdiction you may also have the right to object to or restrict processing, to data portability (GDPR), and to opt out of any "sale" or "sharing" and to non-discrimination (CCPA/CPRA). Indian users have all rights granted under the DPDP Act 2023, including grievance redressal via our Grievance Officer.

8. Age

Known is for users aged 18 and above only.

9. Contact

Grievance Officer: grievance@thewall.app · 7-day response.
