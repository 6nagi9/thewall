1. Executive Summary & Core Value Proposition"The Wall" is a social and professional intelligence platform designed to facilitate pre-meeting and post-meeting interpersonal insights. It enables users to understand a contact's behavioral history, professional reputation, and communication style before or after an interaction, while also serving as a mechanism for individual self-reflection.To prevent the platform from becoming a vector for targeted harassment, slandering, or misinformation, the system utilizes a DEDP-safe architecture—applying structured Data Engineering Design Patterns (DEDP) to enforce strict data contracts, identify anomaly patterns (such as Sybil or collusion attacks), and protect user privacy while ensuring absolute data integrity.1.1 Primary Use CasesPre-Meeting Prep: A user looks up an upcoming contact’s "Wall" via their mobile number to understand their meeting mannerisms, promptness, and reliability based on aggregated, anonymous peer ratings.Post-Meeting Feedback: After a meeting, users quickly rate their experience using pre-structured sliding scales and tag selections, adding structured, non-opinionated data to the target's profile.Self-Reflection & Growth: Users claim their own Wall to view anonymized, aggregated constructive feedback from peers, using it as a mirror for personal and professional improvement.Monetization (Paywall Access): While basic metrics are displayed in a masked or summarized format, users must pay a micro-transaction fee (e.g., a small fee to unlock a specific contact's full Wall) or maintain an active subscription to access detailed, unmasked behavioral analysis.2. DEDP-Safe Technical SafeguardsTo comply with global data safety, privacy, and anti-abuse standards, the platform’s data pipeline relies on specific structural patterns:Deterministic Input Contracts (Defamation Prevention): Free-form text entry is highly restricted. The system enforces strict input structures (sliding scale metrics, standardized categorical tag selection) for peer reviews. Users cannot post raw, unvetted qualitative comments without meeting verified peer thresholds.Sybil & Collusion Threat Protection: The ingestion pipeline analyzes incoming ratings for pattern anomalies. Coordinated positive feedback bursts (self-promoting/ballot-stuffing) or sudden negative feedback spikes (slandering/bad-mouthing) from closely related devices, IP ranges, or newly created accounts are flagged and excluded from the public aggregate ratings.On-Off/Intoxication Threat Resolution: To handle cases where a user performs ordinarily to gain trust and then misbehaves (or vice versa), the aggregating system applies a time-decaying forgetting factor. This mathematically ensures that old behavioral trends decay, giving greater weight to recent, verified interactions:
   $$w_i=e^{-\lambda\cdot t_i}$$$$M=\frac{\sum(w_i\cdot r_i)}{\sum w_i}$$(Where $M$ is the aggregated metric, $r_i$ is the rating, and $w_i$ is the decay weight based on age in days $t_i$.)Data Segregation & Anonymity: Reviewer identity is verified via hardware-bound phone authentication (Firebase Auth / Truecaller SDK) to maintain legal accountability and safe-harbor compliance. However, the database design strictly decouples user authentication records from actual review entries using one-way cryptographic hashing on the server side.3. Conceptual System ArchitectureThe technical stack consists of a Flutter client application (iOS/Android) communicating with a Firebase backend (Authentication, Cloud Firestore, Cloud Functions, and Cloud Messaging).Instead of hardcoding deep schemas into the database layers, the data models are organized conceptually into four high-level domains:User Accounts: Manages secure login states, Premium status, and the user's array of unlocked walls.Public Walls: Flat, aggregated profile objects keyed to a cryptographic hash of a phone number. They display normalized numerical scores and tag counts, ensuring raw identity details are masked.Peer Reviews: Anonymized, immutable transactions representing individual ratings. These are decoupled from the reviewer's account to protect privacy.Ledger Transactions: Stores state records for in-app micro-payments and subscription verification.4. Core Functional Workflows4.1 Frictionless Onboarding & Privacy-First SyncThe user registers securely using phone-number verification or Truecaller One-Tap authentication.The client requests read-only permission for the local contacts directory.Privacy-Safe Mapping: To protect user address books, contact phone numbers are immediately hashed on-device before syncing with the backend. The server maps these hashes to determine which contacts already have active Walls, avoiding the storage of raw, unmasked third-party contact lists on the database.4.2 The "Give-to-Get" Viral LoopTo view another user’s profile details for free, a new user must complete a structural review for exactly five distinct contacts from their phone book.When a review is submitted for a non-registered contact, the backend triggers an automated, templated SMS invitation:"Someone from your network posted feedback about your reliability on The Wall. Log in to claim your profile and see your metrics."4.3 Look-up and Paywall MonetizationThe user inputs a phone number or selects an upcoming contact from their linked calendar.The app checks if the target's Wall is currently "unlocked" for this user.If locked, the UI renders a Masked Preview—showing blurred averages and hiding specific tags or comments.Monetization Prompt: To unlock the full profile, the user must choose one of two options:Pay-per-View (Micro-transaction): Charge a small, one-time fee (e.g., $\$0.99$) via Google Play Billing, Apple In-App Purchases, or Stripe.Premium Monthly Subscription: A flat monthly tier offering unlimited lookups.Once a payment is confirmed via secure server-side webhooks, the target phone hash is permanently added to the user's unlocked profiles list.5. Review Integrity & Dispute ManagementTo ensure fairness and prevent platform misuse:Wall Claiming: Any individual can verify ownership of their mobile number to claim their Wall profile.The Dispute Mechanism: Claimed users can flag specific metrics or tags as inaccurate or malicious. Upon filing a dispute, the target review’s visibility is immediately suspended or visually labeled as Under Dispute until evaluated by automated or manual moderation pipelines.Semantic Filters: Any text fields are processed in real-time through an AI-based moderation API to filter out profanity, targeted bullying, or explicit hate speech prior to publication.6.
