# The Wall — GTM Mastery Curriculum

*Your private apprenticeship in marketing & sales, taught by doing — applied at every step to the real launch of The Wall.*

**Mentor brief:** I'm acting as your CMO + VP of Sales. The goal isn't to make you *recite* Kotler or the Challenger Sale — it's to give you the intuition a great operator has: looking at a product and a market and instantly seeing the wedge, the message, the price, the loop, and the close. We build that intuition the only way it's ever actually built — on a real product, making real decisions with real consequences. Ours is The Wall.

**How this works (hybrid model):**

1. Each module is a written lesson (the file you'll find in this folder). Read it. It teaches the classical frameworks *and* the modern practice, names primary sources so you can go deeper, and applies everything live to The Wall.
2. Every module ends with **Decisions & Exercises** — real choices that produce a real GTM artifact (an ICP, a positioning statement, a pricing model, a launch plan). You bring me your answers.
3. I critique them the way a CMO critiques a PMM's work — honestly, with the reasoning exposed — and we fold the result into the actual launch assets. The artifacts accumulate into a working go-to-market for The Wall.
4. We move to the next module only when the current artifact is real. No theory left hanging in the air.

You set the pace. One module per session is the natural rhythm at the depth we agreed on.

## The arc — why this order

A go-to-market is built in a specific sequence because each layer depends on the one beneath it. You can't write a message until you know who it's for. You can't price until you know the value and the customer. You can't pick channels until you know the message and the unit economics. You can't sell until you can position. We build bottom-up.

### Phase I — Foundations: know the market and the human

**Module 1 — Market, Customer & Jobs-to-be-Done**
Segmentation, targeting (Kotler's STP), Jobs-to-be-Done (Christensen, Ulwick), and the Ideal Customer Profile. Who is The Wall *for*, what job do they hire it to do, and which beachhead we attack first. *Artifact: segment map + primary ICP + the core "job."*

**Module 2 — Positioning & Category Design**
April Dunford's positioning framework, competitive reference frames, category creation vs. category entry, and the "anti-Sarahah" wedge. Where The Wall sits in the customer's mind and against whom. *Artifact: a positioning statement and competitive frame.*

### Phase II — The message

**Module 3 — Value Proposition & Messaging Architecture**
Kotler's value equation, the messaging hierarchy (value prop → pillars → proof), message-market fit, and the craft of persuasion (Ogilvy, Hopkins, Cialdini). Turning positioning into words that move people. *Artifact: messaging house + hero copy for the site and store listing.*

**Module 4 — Brand & Narrative**
Story as strategy, brand voice, the founding narrative, and why "consent-first / honest feedback" is a *story* not a feature list. *Artifact: brand narrative + voice guide.*

### Phase III — The economics

**Module 5 — Pricing & Packaging**
Value-based pricing, the value metric, Van Westendorp & willingness-to-pay, freemium design, and the psychology of the paywall. We pressure-test Wall's free/premium split. *Artifact: pricing & packaging model.*

**Module 6 — Unit Economics & the Funnel Model**
LTV, CAC, payback, contribution margin, and how K-factor changes the whole equation. The spreadsheet that tells you whether a channel can ever work. *Artifact: a working unit-economics & funnel model.*

### Phase IV — Growth & acquisition

**Module 7 — Growth Loops & Product-Led Growth**
Viral loops vs. funnels, K-factor mechanics, the AARRR pirate metrics, and a deep teardown of Wall's escrow/give-to-get engine (building on your existing GROWTH.md). *Artifact: instrumented loop diagram + the P0 fix list, prioritized.*

**Module 8 — Channels & Acquisition Strategy**
Channel selection (the Bullseye framework), organic vs. paid, ASO, content/SEO, influencer, and India-first / WhatsApp distribution. Which channels to test first and how. *Artifact: a channel test plan with budgets and success criteria.*

**Module 9 — Activation, Retention & Lifecycle**
Onboarding design, the "aha moment," Nir Eyal's Hooked model, notification/lifecycle marketing, and cohort retention. Where Wall's give-5 gate leaks and how to fix activation. *Artifact: activation funnel + lifecycle messaging map.*

### Phase V — Sales

**Module 10 — Sales Foundations & Methodology**
The anatomy of a sale, SPIN selling (Rackham), the Challenger Sale (Dixon/Adamson), MEDDIC qualification, and building a repeatable sales process. The discipline behind the deal. *Artifact: a sales process + qualification framework.*

**Module 11 — The B2B Motion for Wall Teams**
Turning Circles into a Teams product, the land-and-expand motion, the demo, objection handling, pricing a B2B tier, and the first 10 customers. *Artifact: B2B GTM playbook + outreach sequence.*

### Phase VI — Orchestration

**Module 12 — Launch Strategy & GTM Orchestration**
Launch tiers, the PR/press play (the "anti-Sarahah" story is a gift), sequencing the launch, and assembling everything into one coherent go-to-market. *Artifact: a dated launch plan.*

**Module 13 — Measurement, Experimentation & the Growth OS**
The metrics that matter by stage, the North Star, experiment design and statistical literacy, dashboards, and building a growth operating system so the machine improves itself. *Artifact: metrics framework + experiment backlog.*

## The reading shelf (optional, for going deeper)

I'll reference these in context; you never *have* to read them, but they're the primary sources behind the intuition.

- **Positioning:** April Dunford, *Obviously Awesome*; Ries & Trout, *Positioning*.
- **Strategy/marketing canon:** Philip Kotler, *Marketing Management*; Byron Sharp, *How Brands Grow*.
- **Jobs-to-be-Done:** Clayton Christensen, *Competing Against Luck*; Tony Ulwick, *Jobs to Be Done*.
- **Copy & persuasion:** David Ogilvy, *Ogilvy on Advertising*; Claude Hopkins, *Scientific Advertising*; Robert Cialdini, *Influence*.
- **Growth:** Sean Ellis & Morgan Brown, *Hacking Growth*; Andrew Chen, *The Cold Start Problem*; Nir Eyal, *Hooked*.
- **Sales:** Neil Rackham, *SPIN Selling*; Dixon & Adamson, *The Challenger Sale*; Aaron Ross, *Predictable Revenue*.
- **Pricing:** Madhavan Ramanujam, *Monetizing Innovation*.
