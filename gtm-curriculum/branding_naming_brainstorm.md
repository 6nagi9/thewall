# Naming & Rebrand Brainstorm — The Wall → ?

*A CMO's working doc. Naming is downstream of positioning, so treat this as a shortlist to finalize after Module 2 (Positioning) — not a final lock. But pre-launch is the cheapest time to rename, so the analysis is worth doing now.*

---

## The criteria a great consumer-app name must hit

Score every candidate against these. The best name isn't the one you love most — it's the one that wins on the most axes, especially **availability**, which kills more "perfect" names than anything else.

1. **Emotional fit** — does it evoke the *job* (to be seen / known honestly and safely)? Emotional names beat descriptive ones for viral consumer products.
2. **Distinctive & ownable** — can you actually own it in search, the app store, and trademark? Plain dictionary words (Wall, Known, Mirror) are emotionally strong but legally and SEO-weak — everyone's fighting for them.
3. **Say-able / spell-able** — one or two syllables, works over the noise of a college canteen, spells itself, no ambiguity in Hindi/regional pronunciation.
4. **Viral mechanics** — does it work *as a verb or a hook*? "Ask me on ___," "get ___," "be ___." NGL ("not gonna lie") and Sarahah won because the name *was* the invite.
5. **Scalable** — doesn't box you into the beachhead (avoid "CollegeRate") or one feature.
6. **Safe across languages** — no unfortunate meaning in Hindi/Urdu/regional tongues; positive connotation.

## Verdict on "The Wall" (the incumbent)

**Strengths:** a real metaphor (build your reputation brick by brick), and you've already built visual equity around it — the brick mark, the "claim your wall" language, the laying-bricks animation on the site.

**Why I'd still move off it:**
- **Hopelessly overloaded.** Facebook Wall (dated), Pink Floyd, Game of Thrones, Wall Street, "the wall" (a barrier). You will never own search or trademark "wall."
- **Wrong emotional vector.** A wall is a *barrier* — something you hide behind. Your product is about *openness and being seen.* The core metaphor fights the core feeling.
- **Domain's already a problem** — your own GROWTH.md notes `thewall.app` isn't configured and the invite links are dead. That's the universe telling you the name isn't ownable.

The brick equity is shallow (pre-launch, no users), so abandoning it now costs almost nothing.

---

## Verdict on "Known" (your proposal)

**It's genuinely good** — possibly the best pure-emotional choice on the board. The deepest human need your product serves is *to be truly known/seen by the people around you.* "Be Known." It's calm, premium, intimate, and modern — which also does positioning work: it sounds like the *safe, grown-up* opposite of edgy anonymous apps (Sarahah/NGL/Tellonym). That contrast is exactly your wedge.

**The catch — availability.** "Known" is a common English word, so:
- `known.com` / `known.app` are almost certainly taken or expensive.
- App Store / Play search will bury you among unrelated results.
- Trademarking a descriptive dictionary word in a crowded class is hard.
- A CMS called "Known" (Withknown) already exists — minor, different category, but noise.

**The fix if you love it:** pair it with a handle that *is* ownable — display name "Known," domain like `getknown.app` / `known.social` / `beknown.app`, store name "Known — honest feedback." Many great apps do exactly this (the legal/searchable name ≠ the spoken brand). Workable. Just go in with eyes open.

## The brainstorm — organized by territory

Naming gets better when you explore *territories* (emotional directions) rather than random words. Six territories, mapped to the job.

### Territory A — "To be seen / known" (the emotional bullseye)
The core payoff. Calm, intimate, premium.

- **Known** — see above. Strong, availability-hard.
- **Seen** — "feeling seen" is the exact payoff; ultra-short. Risk: "left on seen" (read-receipt) carries a faint sting.
- **Mira** — "look" (Spanish) *and* a beloved Indian name (Meera). Warm, India-resonant, short, soft. Risk: used by some other apps; reads as a person's name.
- **Facet** — every facet of you, reflected back; quiet gem metaphor. Brandable and more ownable. Risk: a touch abstract/cerebral for gen-z.

### Territory B — Honesty / candor (the brand promise)
Leans into "honest feedback." These can double as the *invite hook*.

- **Honestly** — wholesome cousin of NGL. Works perfectly as a hook: *"Honestly? You're a great listener."* Conversational, warm, on-brand. Risk: long-ish, availability.
- **Frankly** — same idea, more personality, less crowded than "Honestly." *"Frankly — what do you think of me?"*
- **Candor** — sophisticated word for honest speech; ownable-ish. Risk: skews mature; a 19-year-old won't say "let's candor."
- **Level** — "level with me" idiom; short, modern, confident. Risk: generic (gaming/levels connotation).
- **NoFilter** — instantly communicates raw honesty, very gen-z. Risk: Instagram-filter association; can read as edgy/negative (the thing you're trying *not* to be).

### Territory C — The mirror / reflection (ties to your self-vs-others chart)
Your product literally renders "how you see yourself vs. how others see you" (the Johari-window chart). A mirror name makes that the brand.

- **Aaina / Aina** — Hindi/Urdu for *mirror.* Beautiful, culturally rich, India-first, poetic, and almost nobody's using it in tech. Strong dark-horse if India is the heartland. Risk: pronunciation for a future global audience.
- **Mirror** — perfect metaphor, totally unownable (fitness brand, Black Mirror, generic).
- **Reflect / Reflekt** — clear, but "Reflectly" (a journaling app) already owns adjacent ground.

### Territory D — The ask / invite (the viral surface)
NGL and Sarahah won on the *ask*. A name that *is* the prompt.

- **AskAround** — playful, communicates the social ask. Risk: a bit long/literal.
- **TrueTo** — "be true to you / true to your circle." Compact, ownable-feeling.
- **Realer** — gen-z energy ("keep it realer"). Risk: trendy, may date fast.

### Territory E — Build / construction (keep the metaphor, upgrade the word)
If you want to retain the "built piece by piece" idea without the baggage of "wall."

- **Mosaic** — you're assembled from many people's honest pieces; warm, premium. Risk: used in analytics/enterprise tools.
- **Kiln** — where bricks are *fired/forged*; distinctive, ownable, masculine-cool. Risk: obscure word, needs explaining.
- **Cornerstone** — solid, trust-laden. Risk: corporate/HR connotation.

### Territory F — India-first flavor (if the heartland is the brand)
Your code is India-first (DPDP, Hindi, WhatsApp). A name with desi warmth can be a moat — locally beloved, hard for a US clone to copy.

- **Sach** — Hindi/Urdu for *truth.* Short, punchy, meaningful, ownable. Risk: global pronunciation/spelling later.
- **Sahi** — "right / correct / spot-on" — colloquial and warm. Risk: very informal.
- **Aaina** — (also in Territory C) the standout here.

## My recommendation as your CMO

Three names are genuinely launch-grade, and which one wins depends on a positioning decision we haven't formally made yet:

- **If the brand voice is calm, premium, and emotionally deep → `Known`.** Best feeling, but commit to a creative domain/handle (getknown.app, beknown) because the literal one is gone.
- **If the brand voice is warm, playful, conversational, and the *ask* is the hero → `Honestly` (or `Frankly`).** These names *do the marketing for you* — they're the invite prompt and the brand in one word. My pick if the college beachhead and the viral ask-link are the strategy.
- **If India is unapologetically the heartland → `Aaina`.** The most ownable and the most defensible against a US copycat; a real moat. Bold, and my favorite *contrarian* choice.

**The one-line CMO verdict:** move off "The Wall" — it's overloaded, fights your own emotional promise, and isn't ownable. Between the leaders, I lean **`Honestly`** for a viral-college launch and **`Aaina`** if you want a defensible India-first identity, with **`Known`** as the premium-feel alternative. But we should make this call *with* the positioning, not before it.

## Before anything is final — the availability gate

No name is real until it survives:
1. **Domain** — is a clean, short URL available/affordable?
2. **App stores** — does the name collide with existing apps in search?
3. **Trademark** — is the class clear (esp. in India + US)?
4. **Social handles** — IG / X / TikTok handle available?
5. **Linguistic check** — no bad meaning in Hindi/regional languages.
